I encounter systems that are not working — a city overheating, a suburb without infrastructure, a school without a science magazine. Then I try to understand them rigorously, and change them.

About

I grew up in Kolkata — a city that satellite data shows has been getting measurably hotter for two decades. My first serious research mapped that heat island effect across the city, tracing a strong inverse relationship between urban greenery and surface temperature, with consequences concentrated in the communities least equipped to adapt.

From there, I founded ParticiPlan: a participatory infrastructure initiative that took field research into over a hundred suburban families and translated their needs into costed, technically grounded proposals — securing CSR funding. The premise is simple and underproven: infrastructure works better when the people who live with it help design it.

I am a Senior at Calcutta International School completing the IB Diploma, with Higher Level courses in Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics, and English Language & Literature — a combination that reflects a refusal to choose between the analytical and the humanistic. I am drawn to journalism, environmental work, data science, and research, and most compelled by the space where all four converge.

Research & Projects
01

Decoding the Urban Heat Island Effect in Kolkata

Geospatial Analysis Remote Sensing Environmental Science Two-Decade Study

A longitudinal analysis (2005–2024) of land surface temperatures and vegetation cover across Kolkata using satellite imagery, examining how the urban heat island effect has shifted across seasons and neighbourhoods over two decades.

"Heat zones have significantly intensified as vegetation declined — with a strong inverse relationship between temperature and green cover, concentrated in communities least equipped to adapt."

ParticiPlan

Participatory Design Field Research Infrastructure CSR Funding Secured

A suburban infrastructure remediation project built around direct community engagement. Led field research with over 100 families, translating their expressed needs into costed, technically grounded infrastructure proposals. Secured CSR funding for implementation.

"Infrastructure is far more effective and sustainable when communities co-create solutions. This model proves that participatory, climate-conscious planning can be both scalable and results-driven."
